PhD Project Portfolio

Introduction

My PhD research, conducted in collaboration with Norscot as part of a Knowledge Transfer Partnership (KTP) and supervised by Prof. Farzad Rahimian, explored integrating Building Information Modelling (BIM), Virtual Reality (VR), and gamification to enhance design processes and communication within the Timber Frame Self-Build Housing Sector (TFSBS). The work focused on creating interactive, game-like environments that empower Norscot’s clients and small construction firms to visualise, configure, and engage with sustainable housing projects.

Research Objectives

  • Develop an interactive VR-based house configurator integrating BIM data for design automation and customisation.
  • Bridge the “black box” effect in BIM by exposing metadata and enabling real-time interaction with building components and cost information.
  • Facilitate collaboration between clients, designers, and manufacturers through immersive experiences that simplify complex construction processes.

Key Achievements

  • Designed VR prototypes such as Nisbet and Souter, which improved spatial awareness for Norscot’s clients, saved clients £5,500, and contributed to winning a £53,000 project.
  • Developed a dynamic house configurator in Unreal Engine 4, integrating Revit BIM data and advanced scripting to support automated workflows and price calculation for Norscot’s design team.
  • Presented at major conferences (CIB78, Digital Construction Week) and published in Automation in Construction, contributing to knowledge transfer between academia and industry.
  • Generated annual labour cost savings of approximately £70,000 for Norscot by developing in-house VR/BIM expertise rather than outsourcing.
  • Received the "Innovator of the Future" Commendation at the Scottish Knowledge Exchange Awards 2020, recognising exceptional contributions to innovation.
  • Awarded an Outstanding KTP Grade, the highest possible rating, for the successful project delivery, impact, and innovation.

Prototypes & Tools

Utilised Autodesk Revit and Unreal Engine 4 with Blueprints and C++ scripting to develop interactive configurators featuring:

  • Dynamic teleport menus and object manipulation.
  • Real-time metadata extraction and integration.
  • Automated cost calculations and design scenario testing.
  • Multi-user collaboration and immersive VR walkthroughs.

Final Reflections

This research delivered a novel XR-BIM integration framework for Norscot that enhances client decision-making and reduces design iterations. Conducted as part of a KTP and supervised by Prof. Farzad Rahimian, it also strengthened my project management, research methodology, and communication skills through extensive industry engagement and knowledge dissemination. I look forward to applying these insights to future digital twin projects and supporting sustainable construction practices with Norscot and beyond.
